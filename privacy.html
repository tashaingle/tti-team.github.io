2. Information We Collect

We only collect personal information that you choose to give us, or that is generated when you use our site.

2.1 Information you provide directly

When you contact us or request a quote via the website, or contact us by email, phone or WhatsApp, we may collect:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Telephone number (if you choose to provide it)
  • Property location (town / area)
  • Property details (e.g. size, number of rooms, type of B&B)
  • Estimated number of guest stays per month (if known)
  • Services you are interested in
  • Any other information you include in your message

Our contact and quote forms are currently processed via FormSubmit (formsubmit.co), which sends your form entries to us by email.

2.2 Automatically collected information

Our website hosting platform and service providers may automatically collect basic technical information, such as:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Device type and operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on the site

We currently do not use analytics tools such as Google Analytics or tracking cookies on this site. If this changes in the future, we will update this policy and, where required, ask for your consent.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information to:

  • Respond to your enquiries and messages
  • Provide a quote for our services
  • Communicate with you about potential or ongoing services
  • Manage and deliver B&B and property management services you ask us to provide
  • Keep basic records of enquiries and quotes for business administration
  • Comply with any legal or regulatory obligations

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

4. Legal Bases for Processing (UK GDPR)

We rely on the following legal bases to process your personal information:

  • Legitimate interests: to respond to your enquiry, provide quotes, follow up on potential services and manage our relationship with you as a property owner or business contact.
  • Performance of a contract (or steps before entering into one): where we need to process your information to provide you with a quote or to manage services you have asked us to deliver.
  • Legal obligation: to keep records required by law, e.g. accounting and tax records.

If we ever rely on consent (for example, for optional marketing emails), we will ask you clearly, and you can withdraw that consent at any time.

5. Who We Share Your Information With

We may share your personal information with:

  • Service providers who help us run our website and handle enquiries, such as:
    • FormSubmit (for processing form submissions and emailing them to us)
    • Email and website hosting providers
  • Professional advisers such as accountants or legal advisers, where necessary.
  • Law enforcement or regulators where we are legally required to do so, or where it is necessary to protect our rights.

We only share the minimum information needed and require service providers to keep your information secure and to use it only for providing their services to us.

6. International Transfers

Some of our service providers (for example, email or form processing services) may store or process data outside the UK/EEA. Where this happens, we aim to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as standard contractual clauses or other recognised data protection mechanisms.

7. How Long We Keep Your Information

We keep your personal information only for as long as it is reasonably necessary for the purposes set out in this policy, including:

  • To respond to your enquiry or quote request
  • To consider and provide our services
  • To comply with legal, accounting or reporting obligations

In many cases, this means we may keep enquiry and quote records for up to 6 years after our last contact with you, unless a longer period is required by law or needed for potential legal claims.

We will delete or anonymise information when it is no longer needed.

8. How We Protect Your Information

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information, including:

  • Using reputable service providers for website hosting, forms and email
  • Limiting access to personal data to those who need it for legitimate business purposes
  • Using password protection and other reasonable security practices

However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. While we do our best to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Your Rights

Under UK data protection law, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Request deletion of your personal information in certain circumstances
  • Object to our processing where we rely on legitimate interests
  • Restrict how we process your information in certain circumstances
  • Request a copy of your data in a portable format (where applicable)

If you would like to exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in section 1. We may ask for additional information to confirm your identity before responding.
